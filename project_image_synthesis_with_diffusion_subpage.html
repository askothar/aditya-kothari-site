Project

Image Synthesis with Diffusion Models

UNet‑based Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Model (DDPM) trained on Fashion‑MNIST and Flowers‑102 under 4GB GPU constraints; sample quality, training curves, and ablations.

Abstract

We implement a UNet‑backed DDPM for unconditional image generation at 32×32 resolution. Training pipelines are constructed for Fashion‑MNIST and Flowers‑102, with ablations over noise schedules and sampling steps. We analyze FID‑style trends (proxy) and visual fidelity across checkpoints, noting resource‑aware tradeoffs and avenues for improvement (e.g., classifier‑free guidance and lightweight text conditioning).

Adversarial Attack Method

Model & Noise Schedules

  • UNet with down/up blocks and skip connections; time‑step embeddings via sinusoidal encodings.
  • Linear vs cosine noise schedules; sampling via ancestral DDIM/DPMSolver‑like steps (conceptual comparison).

Datasets & Preprocessing

Fashion‑MNIST (grayscale) and Flowers‑102 (RGB) resized to 32×32; normalization; class‑balanced shuffles; augmentation trials.

Training & Evaluation

  • Loss: mean‑squared error on noise prediction.
  • Tracking: sample grids every N steps; approximate FID trend; reconstruction sanity checks.
  • Constraints: 4GB GPU; tuned batch size and gradient accumulation.

Results

Sample Quality

Progressive improvement in sample sharpness and diversity over epochs; cosine schedule slightly outperformed linear under constraints.
