Project

Collision Avoidance with Fuzzy Logic

Design and evaluation of a Mamdani Type‑1 fuzzy logic controller (FLC) for a warehouse robot, integrated with MATLAB/Simulink and Gazebo across seven scenarios.

⬇ Read Full Report (PDF) ← Back to Projects

Figure: FLC inputs, membership functions, and rule base.

Abstract

We present a fuzzy logic-based collision avoidance controller for mobile robots in structured environments. Inputs include obstacle distance, relative speed, and angle; the Mamdani Type‑1 FLC produces steering and speed commands. The controller is integrated with a MATLAB/Simulink and Gazebo stack and evaluated on seven scenarios, emphasizing safe behavior and recovery in edge cases.

Adversarial Attack Method

Controller Structure

  • Inputs: obstacle distance, relative speed, relative angle.
  • Memberships: triangular/trapezoidal sets tuned via simulation for coverage and smoothness.
  • Inference: Mamdani with centroid defuzzification; outputs mapped to steering and velocity commands.

Simulation Environment

MATLAB/Simulink controller connected to Gazebo world; lidar-based perception feed; logging for safety metrics.

Test Scenarios

Seven layouts varying obstacle density and dynamics; baseline PID or rule-based controller used for comparison.

Results

Safety & Performance

The FLC avoided collisions in stress scenarios where the baseline failed; path smoothness and time‑to‑goal improved under moderate clutter.

Qualitative

Takeaways

  • Fuzzy control offered robust, interpretable behavior without heavy tuning.
  • Future: adaptive memberships, Type‑2 FLC for uncertainty, multi‑objective tuning.
