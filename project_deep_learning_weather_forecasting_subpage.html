Project

Deep Learning Based Weather Forecasting

ConvLSTM and U‑Net models for precipitation nowcasting on the SEVIR dataset; training pipeline, visualization, and analysis of failure modes.

⬇ Read Full Report (PDF) ← Back to Projects

Figure: Qualitative prediction frames.

Abstract

We evaluate deep learning approaches for short‑term precipitation nowcasting using the SEVIR dataset. A U‑Net baseline and a ConvLSTM sequence‑to‑sequence architecture are implemented with identical preprocessing and training regimes. We build a reproducible pipeline to generate qualitative GIFs and compute quantitative metrics across horizons. Observed failure modes with ConvLSTM are analyzed with recommendations for stabilization.

Adversarial Attack Method

Data & Preprocessing

SEVIR radar frames; normalization, cropping, temporal stacking of past frames to predict future precipitation intensity.

Models

  • U‑Net: encoder‑decoder with skip connections for per‑pixel regression.
  • ConvLSTM: sequence model capturing spatiotemporal dynamics across frames.

Training & Eval

  • Loss: MSE/Huber variants; scheduled sampling trials.
  • Metrics: MAE/MSE per horizon; structural similarity on predicted frames.
  • Artifacts: qualitative GIFs for side‑by‑side model comparison.

Results

Quantitative

U‑Net outperformed ConvLSTM under the chosen settings; ConvLSTM displayed training instability leading to degraded forecasts at longer horizons.

Qualitative Frames

Takeaways

  • U‑Net provides strong spatial detail for nowcasting; ConvLSTM needs stabilization.
  • Future: larger context windows, attention mechanisms, and physics‑informed losses.
